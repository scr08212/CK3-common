A modifier is an effect that adds various bonuses or penalties to a dynasty, house, character or county. Modifiers can be temporary or permanent. Characters can have an unlimited number of modifiers but only the first 10 will be displayed.

=== Domes ===
{{expansion|auh}}
Domes are dynasty modifiers that rulers can obtain by paying for the Capital Temple Complex great project contribution with the same name. Gaining a more powerful modifier will remove the weaker one but a weaker modifier is not needed to gain a more powerful one.

=== Warhorse ===
The warhorse is a unique modifier that can gradually increase in effects. It can be obtained as a result of travel, {{iconify|Martial Lifestyle}} or [[File:Wandering lifestyle.png|24px]] Wandering Lifestyle events. If the Royal Court DLC is installed it can also be obtained from royal court events. The modifier will initially grant the following bonuses:
* {{green|+1}} {{iconify|Prowess}}
* {{green|+0.25}} Monthly {{iconify|Prestige}}

The day after the modifier is obtained an event will trigger to give the warhorse a name.

The modifier can be improved in two ways, both costing a small amount of {{iconify|Gold}} and offering a choice of what horse talent to improve. The modifier can only be improved twice.
* Every 275-400 days there is a 15% chance to gain the option to train the warhorse. If the Master of the Horse court position is being used its holder can be asked to do it instead, which removes the {{iconify|Gold}} cost and grants a temporary {{green|+15}} {{iconify|Opinion}} with the Master of the Horse but chooses the training at random. The event can only take place once.
* 25-30 years after obtaining the modifier the warhorse will start to die of old age and offer the option to breed it. Not breeding it will result in the loss of the modifier in 30-60 days.

== Pets ==
Pets are character modifiers that last between 11 and 16 years. They can be inherited if a character dies before the pet but can also be lost through events. Both pets unlock a {{iconify|Decision}} to pet them, have a hidden {{green|33%}} chance to save the character when a Murder Scheme is executed against them, and have a very small chance to expose schemes targeting the character. There are three pet modifiers:
* {{icon|cat}} Pet Cat grants {{green|+1}} to {{iconify|Intrigue}} and {{iconify|Learning}}
* {{icon|dog}} Pet Dog grants {{green|+1}} to {{iconify|Diplomacy}} and {{iconify|Prowess}} and can receive additional events during {{iconify|Hunt}} activities
* [[File:Modifier bird positive.png|24px]] Hunting Eagle grants {{green|+1}} to {{iconify|Martial}} and {{iconify|Stewardship}} and can receive additional events during {{iconify|Hunt}} activities

Pets can be obtained in multiple ways. One day after getting the pet the character will be given the option to input a name for the pet.
* At any point the character may be given a pet from a consort, friend, lover or vassal and gain the option to keep it or sell it for a minor random amount of {{iconify|Gold}}.
* During childhood the character might encounter a pet and have the option to keep it and gain the modifier. Alternatively if the character has the [[File:Trait sadistic.png|24px]] Sadistic trait it can kill the pet to gain {{green|+1}} {{iconify|Learning}}.
* A Pet Dog can be obtained via the Adopt Puppy and Adopt a Kennel Dog decisions.
* If the {{iconify|Royal Court}} DLC is installed a pet can be offered in certain court events.
* If the {{iconify|Wandering Nobles}} DLC is installed a pet can be offered as part of travel events.

A day after obtaining a pet an event will trigger to give the pet a name. While the character has a pet the following events can take place:
* A vassal plays with the pet and gains {{green|+20}} {{iconify|Opinion}}
* The pet brings 2 {{iconify|Gold}}
* The pet gives a temporary {{iconify|Health Boost}}
* The pet ruins a vassal's possessions, bringing the following options:
** Pay the vassal and lose a moderate random amount of {{iconify|Gold}}
** Lose {{red|-20}} {{iconify|Opinion}} with the vassal
* If the character has two pets it might gain an event that grants {{green|-20}} {{icon|stress loss}} Stress
* If the character has two pets they might fight. Within two weeks one on the following will happen:
** 75% chance a pet dies
** 15% chance both pets die
** 10% chance they get along and the character gains {{green|-10}} {{icon|stress loss}} Stress

If both {{iconify|Tours and Tournaments}} and {{iconify|Roads to Power}} DLCs are installed, there are two variants of the [[File:Modifier dog positive.png|24px]] Pet Dog modifier that can be obtained for 25 {{iconify|Gold}} as part of adventurer random events. Both of them increase {{iconify|Hunt}} success chance:
* Beasthound gives {{green|+1}} {{iconify|Martial}} and {{iconify|Prowess}} and {{green|+0.1}} Monthly Prestige
* Scenthound gives {{green|+1}} {{iconify|Diplomacy}} and {{iconify|Learning}} and {{green|+0.1}} Monthly Prestige

{{expansion|roc}}
When a pet dies of old age, if the character has a Royal Court it will have the option to pay 10-20 {{iconify|Gold}} to gain a Small Wall Ornament artifact.
